# CAF-008 – Architecture Language

## Purpose

Define the common architectural language used throughout Crow Architecture Framework (CAF) to ensure consistent communication, documentation and interpretation.

## Objectives

- Establish a common vocabulary
- Promote consistent terminology
- Reduce ambiguity
- Support interoperability between architecture artefacts

## Language Principles

Architecture language shall be precise, technology-neutral where practical, unambiguous and consistently applied across all governed documents.

## Terminology

Core terminology shall be defined in CAF Foundation documents and the CAF Glossary. Normative terms shall have a single authoritative definition.

## Normative Keywords

Normative requirements shall use the keywords SHALL, SHOULD and MAY consistently throughout CAF and CAS documents.

## Naming Conventions

Artefacts shall use unique identifiers, descriptive names and consistent naming conventions to support discoverability and traceability.

## Documentation Rules

Documents shall use consistent structure, headings, metadata and references in accordance with CAF templates and governance.

## Conformance

A CAF-compliant document shall use the defined terminology, normative language and naming conventions established by this standard.

## Summary

Architecture Language establishes the common vocabulary and documentation conventions required for consistent communication throughout the framework.
